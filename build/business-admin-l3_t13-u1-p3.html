Project lifecycle and roles

An example of a work-based project

Projects usually have a beginning, a middle and an end once the idea has been put forward. For example, an event management company’s new project is to run a charity concert for a client:

  • The initial idea is put forward and discussed.
  • At the beginning of the project, the company’s tasks are linked to planning the event.
  • In the middle of the project, the company runs the actual event.
  • At the end of the project, the company works on feedback and evaluation about how the concert went.

Using the charity concert as an example of a project, tasks for managers and their teams could include, for example:

  • researching and planning the project
    eg finding a venue, booking performers
  • working out timescales
    eg planning when things need to be delivered
  • finding and organising resources
    eg extra staff to act as stewards on the day; seating and catering at the venue; ordering portable toilets
  • sending out information about the concert
    eg publicity materials to news agencies and media; putting up posters; arranging TV, radio and press advertisements
  • contacting people coming to the event
    eg sending invitations and tickets; dealing with telephone and email queries
  • setting up the venue and supporting the organisers
    eg providing administration support and dealing with queries
  • helping to host guests and performers
    eg arranging transport or giving directions
  • supporting event staff after the concert
    eg taking care of lost property; taking unused programmes back to the office; dealing with queries
  • evaluating the success of the concert
    eg reviewing ticket sales; responding to feedback, complaints and comments; helping to sort receipts and analyse the costs.

The tasks are focused on the project and follow the plans and objectives of the project. They do not form part of the regular, routine work of the organisation. Once the project is over, the tasks cease.

Working to timescales is critical. Things must be done on time as delays can have a knock-on effect on other people and tasks connected with the project.
