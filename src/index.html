Orginisation types

Introduction

Your organisation will form part of one of three sectors: the public sector, the private sector or the third sector. There are major differences between these three sectors, mainly in their purpose, the way they are run and financed, as well as what they do with any profits.

To understand how your organisation's policies and procedures affect your role, it is important to recognise the differences between the three sectors. Talk to your employer and colleagues about your organisation – they will have an insight into the way its aims relate to its sector.

Key terms

  • Purpose
    the reason for which something exists.
  • Activities
    actions undertaken by organisations, such as buying, selling, marketing or investing, for the purpose of generating profits or developing economic opportunities.
  • Aims
    where the business wants to go in the future, its goals, a statement of resolve.
  • Values
    the operating philosophies or principles that guide an organisation's conduct and its relationship with customers, partners and shareholders.
  • Vision for the future
    where the organisation wants to go rather than its current state.

Orginisation types

Organisations can be grouped in one of three sectors:

  • Private
    organisations that exist to make a profit (eg retailers) and are owned by shareholders which may number from one to thousands; companies whose shares may be bought by the public are identified by the letters plc after their name.
  • Public
    organisations funded by public money (ie taxes), which include hospitals, schools, etc.
  • Not-for-profit
    (the community and voluntary sector) – charitable organisations such as Oxfam.

There are major differences between these three groups or sectors, mainly in terms of their purposes, the way they are run, how they are financed and what they do with their profits or surpluses of cash.
