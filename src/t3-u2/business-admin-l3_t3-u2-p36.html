Interpersonal skills

3 Delivery methods

There are many occasions when we ask questions, so much so that it’s an everyday occurrence. The methods we use to ask questions include a written memo or email, a face-to-face meeting or a telephone call.

Feedback responses vary depending on the method used. In a face-to-face scenario, people can tell you there and then if something doesn’t make sense.

However, asking questions over the phone requires the need to pause or invite feedback.

Stimulate thinking

Questions often generate further questions that help to clarify and broaden understanding.

Generating responses

As well as getting information back, the answers you are given reveal misconceptions and misunderstanding as well as any understanding and knowledge beyond what’s expected.

Making links

This is less about asking for the facts and more about using what people already know to analyse, evaluate and create ideas.
