Interpersonal skills

2 Active and deeper listening

Using active and deeper listening means that the listener has a better understanding of the other person. This in turn means that both the listener and the person talking get the maximum benefit out of the communication. When we actively listen:

  • there is a sense of the feeling that the other person is experiencing behind the words they are saying
  • there is a deeper connection and understanding of each other
  • we have better insights into problems and decisions
  • we use more than our ears â€“ the listener intuitively starts to understand the other person on a deeper level.

Of course there are challenges to doing this. One challenge is time. In order to have the opportunity to build listening skills we need the time that allows the other person to talk without distractions so we can get beyond the initial levels of listening to active listening. Another challenge is environment. To really listen, a quiet environment without interruptions is best.
