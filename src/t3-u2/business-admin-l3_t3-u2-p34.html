Interpersonal skills

Effective questioning

1 Checking knowledge

Effective questioning should be used to check knowledge. This includes your knowledge and understanding of the person’s needs and their understanding of the information you have provided. These techniques can be used:

  • paraphrasing – restate the content of the message in your own words, eg ‘If I heard you correctly, you would like to …’
  • clarifying – review your understanding by asking for clarity, eg ‘When do you want to …?’
  • probing – obtain more information by asking open and closed questions such as ‘Could you give me the date …?’
  • verifying – check for understanding, eg ‘Do you want me to …? Is this right?’
  • summarising – restate the key points of the discussion at the end
  • leading – a question that prompts or encourages the answer wanted.

You should also ask brief questions and paraphrase the person's statements to test for understanding. To help guide and control their interest, use rhetorical questions to confirm understanding, assumptive questions to create decisions, and leading questions to direct them towards a solution.

At its most basic level a question is a sentence or phrase used to obtain information.
