Supply chains

7 Problems that arise in supply chains

There are a number of problems that can arise in supply chains:

  • delays – eg production, distribution, supply
  • raw materials – eg availability and cost
  • inventory costs – eg expensive raw materials, high number of requirements
  • uncertainties – eg deliveries, politics
  • forecasting – eg reliance on historical experience, uncertain demand for new products
  • production delays – eg availability of labour, strikes, staff absences, national holiday variations
  • quality – eg poor production from suppliers
  • complexity – eg the longer the supply chain the larger the network of businesses involved, communication, complexity of products
  • differentiation – eg whether customisation, variations, localised versions of products are supported, and at which stage in the supply chain differentiations are developed
  • decentralisation – eg control of the supply chain is distributed throughout businesses
  • customer service – eg pressure from consumers for transparent supply chains, ethics, fairtrade, removal of slavery and human trafficking from supply chains, sustainability
  • demand – eg variations in demand can be localised or time-bound.
