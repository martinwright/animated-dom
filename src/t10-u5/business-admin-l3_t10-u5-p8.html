Supply chains

4 Supply chain management

Supply chain management can be defined as the active management of supply chain activities to maximise customer value and achieve a sustainable competitive advantage. It involves:

  • organisations, procedures and people
  • activities, eg purchasing, delivery and packaging, warehousing
  • relationship management, eg suppliers, distributors
  • information flow through the supply chain.

Effective supply chain management reduces uncertainty in the network, ensures appropriate inventory levels, minimises delays, eliminates unplanned activities, provides good customer service and involves these main concepts:

  • fulfilment
    eg delivery of the right order to agreed timescales
  • front office operations
    eg order management, advertising and marketing, customer service, customer relationship management
  • back office operations
    eg finance, inventory and stock management, warehousing, packaging and logistics, demand planning
  • logistics
    eg the flow of goods, information and finance along a supply chain.
