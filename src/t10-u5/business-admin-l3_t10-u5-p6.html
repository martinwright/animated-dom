Supply chains

3 Technology push vs market pull

Push based supply chains involve products being developed and ‘pushed’ to market. Levels of production are set by manufacturers based on their historical experience and as a result push based supply chains are slow to respond if demand is greater or lower than expected which can result in overstocking or delays.

Pull based supply chains are demand driven and either respond to specific customer orders or set levels of replenishment. For example, car manufacturers may produce specific models or configurations to order and maintain limited inventory for popular vehicles.

Supply chains are usually a combination of technology push and market pull. For example, a manufacturer may keep an inventory of individual parts but not undertake final assembly of a product until a specific order is received.
