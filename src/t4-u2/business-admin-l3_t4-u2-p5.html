Accuracy and proofing

2 Proofreading

You need to check that spelling is correct:

  • Use a dictionary or spellchecker.
  • Remember ‘homophones’ (eg there/their; tyre/tire; here/hear).
  • Remember basic spelling rules (eg 'i before e except after c', as in ‘friend’ but not in ‘received’).
  • Remember endings for plurals (eg baby/babies, match/matches, potato/potatoes).

You need to check that the right grammar has been used. Remember to use the correct tense:

  • If you wanted to say you are going to the head office tomorrow, make sure you say: ‘I am going to go to the head office tomorrow’ not ‘I am go to the head office’.
  • If you had already been to the head office yesterday it would be: ‘I went to the head office yesterday’.

You need to check that the punctuation is correct:

  • Make sure that you have used capital letters and full stops in your sentences.
  • Check that all proper names (nouns) have a capital letter, for example: Linda, Spain, January.
  • Always check that you have used a capital and not a small i. For example, ‘He said that I would need to go tomorrow’ not ‘He said that i would need to go tomorrow’.
  • Correct your mistakes before the final copy.
  • If there are mistakes in a letter or CV it will look bad.
  • You can spot errors by reading what you’ve written out loud.
