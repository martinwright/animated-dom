Accuracy and proofing

Accuracy matters

1 Introduction

Once you have completed your written document you must check the content for accuracy. You need to check:

  • spelling
  • grammar
  • that information and facts are correct
  • that any hyperlinks work
  • the subject headings relate correctly to the content.

One efficient method is to first produce a rough draft which you can then edit to:

  • check sentences and paragraphs
  • take out unnecessary or repeated words or content
  • apply formatting using sub-headings.

It is often a good idea to take a break so that you can return to it with a fresh eye after letting your brain take a rest. Returning to a document the following day allows you to add extra information or structure it into a different format to suit the audience.

Asking someone else to check your document is a good idea as they can check the document for:

  • errors
  • missing information required to help with context
  • appropriateness for the document's intended audience.

You may choose to print out your document to allow you to review it on paper or to read it aloud to adjust the flow, pacing and rhythm of sentences and words.

Before you save a final version of your document or send an email, it is a good idea to do a final spelling and grammar check (making sure your computer is set to the right language first).
