9 Identifying suitable tasks that have the potential for delegation

Delegating tasks effectively begins with our own self-management. We need to organise our own responsibilities first so that tasks can be prioritised in terms of complexity, deadlines and resources required. To achieve this we can, for example:

  • classify tasks in terms of urgency and importance
  • apply Paretoâ€™s 80/20 rule
  • create ABC123 lists of tasks
  • put an action plan together.

Once the tasks are clear, we can identify those that could be delegated. We might consider:

  • whether the task is routine or a one-off
  • if the task is highly defined or one that has less structure
  • if the task is complex or simple
  • the experience, knowledge, skills and behaviours required for the task
  • whether the task requires leadership/management skills
  • who will be accountable and responsible
  • whether decision making about the task can be fully delegated
  • if the task offers the team or team members development and growth
  • the stakeholders involved in or affected by the task.

This will help us to understand whether a task is one that is suitable for delegation and may identify who might be able to carry it out.
