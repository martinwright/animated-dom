Effective teamwork

10 Identifying potential team members or teams suitable for the task

There are a number of factors to consider when identifying who might be suitable to take on a delegated task. These include:

  • the person’s and/or team’s experience to date
  • whether people or teams have done similar tasks previously
  • the knowledge, skills, expertise and competencies within the team
  • the competence and commitment shown when doing similar tasks in the past
  • the different contributions made
  • current workloads and availability of team members
  • the willingness of team members to take on new responsibilities
  • people’s previous experience of delegated tasks
  • whether it is a particular person’s ‘turn’ to undertake a task.

In order to build a clearer picture of who we might delegate the task to we could also use:

  • competency and skills matrices
  • outcomes from appraisals
  • one to ones and job chats
  • actions from personal development plans.
