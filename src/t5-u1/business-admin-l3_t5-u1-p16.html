Effective teamwork

2 Charles Handy

Charles Handy (1993 p328) describes delegation as the dilemma that exists between trust and control. Trust relates to the team member and how much trust he or she believes the manager has in them. Control relates to the manager and the level of control he or she has over the work to be done.

According to Handy, effective delegation requires a balance to be struck so that once a task is delegated sufficient control is maintained by the manager, but that control does not erode the trust felt by the team member to get on with the task.

The implication here is the situational nature of delegation and the different factors that affect what is delegated and by how much.
