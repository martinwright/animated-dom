If a task for delegation requires presenting and report writing, for example, it is simple to identify where the strengths in the team lie. However, it may also be an opportunity for another member of the team to develop their presentation skills. Whilst they may not have a lot of experience in this area they may relish the opportunity to develop their expertise.

Outcomes from appraisals – annual appraisals, if used effectively, offer a great opportunity to identify areas where team members may want to take on more responsibility or new tasks. The nature of appraisals can uncover a team member’s willingness to develop, to take on new projects.

One to ones and job chats – although less formal than appraisals, these are still an opportunity for a manager and team member to discuss new tasks and potential delegation opportunities.

Actions from personal development plans – these may identify new skills or areas of learning that a team member has undertaken. They may be keen to have the opportunity to put these new skills into practice.
