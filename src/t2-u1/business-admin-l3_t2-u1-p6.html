3. How will I get there?

This is where the practical details of your plan start to come together and you find learning activities and other ways to improve yourself and your performance at work â€“ eg ask for a career appraisal with a manager; take various courses; do voluntary work to gain extra experience; offer to learn new tasks at work; shadow experienced colleagues to learn new skills; discuss the best training options with managers, colleagues or other training providers.

During the process, you focus on your personal goals and set targets that are specific to you and your needs. You prepare a personal development plan (PDP) to bring all of this together and record progress. By following a structured process, chances of success are increased, and you can have a permanent record to show your progress, achievements, problems and ideas. These are useful for you to review and show others who are involved in your career development, promotion plans or training programmes.
