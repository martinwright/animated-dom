Coaching and mentoring

Coaching

Coaching involves conducting a purposeful conversation, focused on one or more challenges or outcomes, stimulated by insightful and probing questions – rather than by directly offering answers and solutions.

Coaching definitions:

  • is essentially a non-directive form of development
  • focuses on improving performance and developing individuals’ skills
  • personal issues may be discussed but the emphasis is on performance at work
  • coaching activities have both organisational and individual goals
  • it provides people with feedback on both their strengths and their weaknesses
  • usually coaching is short term.

In reality there is a lot of overlap between coaching and mentoring to the extent that a ‘coaching’ approach is often taken within mentoring. After all, the above coaching definitions are really, what an appointed mentor would be seeking to achieve too!

Coaching and mentoring may focus on:

  • skills
  • behaviours
  • decision making
  • fundamental beliefs
  • values
  • overall performance.

Coaching has numerous applications. Some of the more obvious being:

  • accelerating and enhancing performance
  • focused development of potential
  • for handling complex problems
  • addressing individual and organisational change
  • support personal transformation
  • career role transition
  • development of leaders
  • facilitating the implementation of a learning culture.
