Coaching and mentoring

Mentoring

The term mentoring comes from the Greek myth where Odysseus entrusts the education of his son to his friend Mentor.

In the workplace, mentoring describes the situation where more experienced colleagues support the development of junior or inexperienced staff to improve their knowledge and understanding of the work or workplace.

The characteristics of mentoring include:

  • It is essentially a supportive form of development.
  • It focuses on helping an individual manage their career and improve their skills.
  • Personal issues can be discussed more productively unlike in coaching where the emphasis is usually on performance at work.
  • Mentoring activities have both organisational and individual goals.

Mentoring is applicable in several different contexts including:

  • situational mentoring: help just when you need it
  • informal mentoring: nurtured over time
  • formal mentoring: with a defined beginning and end.
