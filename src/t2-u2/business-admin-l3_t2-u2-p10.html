3 Monitoring of individuals’ development

Compilation of individual development plans is not of course an end in itself. The end product is the successful implementation of an individual’s development at work. The PDP is a ‘living’ document supporting an ongoing process, which requires monitoring and review. Regular and planned review of progress against the agreed objectives within a PDP helps to maintain momentum towards timely completion of each development activity. Even the best of PDPs will flounder if you don’t set in place the expectation and means for implementation and then ensure the development happens. Monitoring progress may be a simple matter of meeting with the employee to discuss and amend the plan, but the level of accountability you expect from the individual needs to be clearly defined.

Where performance improvement is concerned, significant resources are involved or where the development plan results are business critical, you may decide to play a more active role in monitoring progress.

Methods of monitoring progress include:

  • periodic meetings to check progress
  • requiring a written update from the individual as each objective is completed or if problems materialise
  • requiring the individual to update the PDP on a regular basis
  • obtaining feedback from a training and development provider.
