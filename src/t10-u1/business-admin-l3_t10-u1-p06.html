3 Local authorities

Local authorities are responsible for the running of local areas. There are several types:

  • County and district councils – county councils cover large areas and provide most public services, including schools, social services and public transportation; district councils cover smaller areas within the county and provide more local services, including council housing, gyms and leisure facilities, local planning, recycling and rubbish collection.
  • Unitary authorities – in most large towns and cities there will be just one level of local government called a 'unitary authority'. They are responsible for all local services, instead of the two-tier system of county and district councils.
  • Town and parish councils – exist in some parts of England and Wales covering small areas, and with responsibility for, eg allotments, public toilets, parks, war memorials and local community centres.

Some local authorities ‘share’ services covering a wider area, like police, fire services and public transport, because they may be too small to run an effective service on their own. This is known as joint services.
