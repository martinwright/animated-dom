5 Public limited company (PLC)

Public limited companies are often large well-known organisations such as manufacturers or retail chains. Their shares are traded on the London Stock Exchange and they become a plc by offering their shares for sale to the general public through flotations. Public limited companies must:

  • have at least two directors, one of whom must be a company secretary
  • meet minimum share capital and paid up capital requirements
  • produce annual accounts and reports
  • comply with the London Stock Exchange accounting principles and conditions and processes for the trading of shares
  • submit to a due diligence investigation.
