The third sector

1 Introduction

How many voluntary organisations can you name?

Voluntary sector organisations undertake social activity as their aim and are not for profit. They are sometimes referred to as the community or third sector. This sector also includes organisations which operate under charitable status, but whose income is invested back into the operation rather than being raised on behalf of a cause, eg City & Guilds.

Charitable organisations are a type of non-profit organisation (NPO). They differ from other types of NPOs in that they centre on non-profit and philanthropic (love of humanity) goals as well as social well-being (eg charitable, educational, religious or other activities serving the public interest or common good). Charities are registered at the Charities Commission.

The government offers advice about setting up a charity and how non-profit organisations can set up social enterprise businesses that have social, charitable or community-based objectives.

There are four common charitable structures:

  • unincorporated associations
  • charitable trusts
  • charitable incorporated organisations
  • charitable companies.
