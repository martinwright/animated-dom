Orginisation types

2 Sole trader

If you start working for yourself, you’re classed as a self-employed sole trader – even if you’ve not yet told HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). Sole traders run their own businesses as individuals and can keep all the business’s profits after paying tax on them. Sole traders can employ staff and are responsible for the business, and are personally responsible for any losses the business makes. For example, if a window cleaner is set up as a sole trader and breaks something expensive in someone’s house the householder has the right to sue the sole trader for the cost of the item. If the sole trader does not have the cash, then personal assets can be seized, ie a car or house.

The legal requirements to set up as a sole trader are to:

  • register the business name with the National Business Register
  • register as self-employed with HM Revenue & Customs.
