4 Public sector organisations

The main objectives of any organisation in the public sector is to provide efficient and effective public services, whilst carrying out government policy and complying with legislation. As they are funded with public taxes, they must provide the best value for money services. Usually, an organisation’s aim is stated in its mission statement. Private sector organisations usually focus on the customer. Public sector organisations usually concentrate on the service they offer. Look at the following mission statements:

Ultimately, a public sector organisation must provide a service to the general public as directed by the central government department that determines the overall policy that organisations must work to, eg health policy is determined by central government and interpreted and implemented by the National Health Service. Therefore, if the central government policy is to make cuts, then local organisations will be forced to streamline their operations to comply. This is particularly noticeable in the area of education, where different governments have brought in different policy on, eg the curriculum or the minimum age for leaving education and training. The schools and training providers at the bottom of the structure must then implement this policy.
