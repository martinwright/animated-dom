6 Contingency plans

Part of project management planning is to make contingency plans to cover unforeseen costs, last-minute problems and unforeseen events or issues that have an impact on the project. In our example, the organisers would have contingency plans for extreme weather around the time of the concert, for example: to provide shelter and water for crowds queuing for hours out in very hot sun; to make sure that cars all park on hard-standing areas or have access to metal trackway if extreme rain affects conditions.

Project managers need to have a contingency budget set aside to cover their contingency plans. This makes it easier to make quick decisions to get the project back on track if funds, and limits of authority to use them, have been agreed in advance.
