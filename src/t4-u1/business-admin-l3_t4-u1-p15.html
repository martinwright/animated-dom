5 Assess activities using the Pareto Principle

The Pareto Principle, also known as the 80-20 rule, is a method we can use to assess efforts and activities to see which are the most productive. This can be applied to time management and many other aspects of business â€“ eg planning, decision making, leadership or project management.

General examples of the Pareto Principle include:

  • 80% of output is produced from 20% of input
  • 80% of results come from 20% of effort
  • 80% of profit comes from 20% of the product range.

When we review our activity log, we should be able to identify our most productive 20% of effort that produces 80% of our output. By standing back and analysing this, we can plan how to improve our general output and efficiency.
