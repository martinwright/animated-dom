Developing a workplan

In this section we are going to explore the subject of workload planning and different approaches to carrying out this key component of being effective in the workplace. We will consider:

  • sources of information needed for effective workload planning
  • different approaches to planning workload
  • the importance of planning ahead.

The online business dictionary’s definition of workload is:

‘Amount of work or number of work units assigned to a particular resource over a given period.’

and their definition of planning is:

‘A basic management function involving formulation of one or more detailed plans to achieve optimum balance of needs or demands with the available resources.’

Successful workload planning means managers achieve a balance between the amount of work to be done and the time and resources available. This is an ongoing process as work, resources and timeframes change, resulting in the need to review and adjust plans accordingly.
