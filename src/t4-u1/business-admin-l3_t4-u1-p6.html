3 The importance of planning ahead

A key management function is to be able to plan. Plans often need to be adjusted as a result of unexpected changes, however strong planning provides everybody with a detailed understanding of what needs to be done, by whom and by when. This also applies to planning our workload.

Therefore planning ahead helps to:

  • Set direction – starting in a clear and well-defined direction means that when changes do occur they are managed to maintain the progress to the agreed goal.
  • Allocate resources – means that people, time and budgets can be allocated to support necessary work and avoid any shortfalls.
  • Manage risks – careful planning will highlight any potential risks to work in the future allowing for contingency planning.
  • Create certainty – planning ahead will create a sense of certainty about what is to be done in order to achieve goals and objectives.
  • Improve decision making – knowing the direction aimed for provides a clear context to any decisions made in the future.
  • Promote efficient working – will highlight potential areas of waste and can therefore promote a more efficient approach to work that focuses on the key tasks.
  • Ensure tasks are prioritised – tasks can be evaluated against the direction that has been set and then properly prioritised based on their level of importance.
  • Avoid diary and scheduling conflicts – taking into account activities already in the diary means that new plans can be made to avoid conflicts.
  • Support the delivery of projects – often ‘one-off’ projects have to be integrated into normal operations – planning ahead will ensure that where projects have to be accommodated, this can be done without upsetting normal operations.
