Change in the workplace

5 Beckhard and Harris’ formula for change

Another model for change management is Beckhard and Harris’ formula for change. Based on theories developed by David Gleicher, the formula was refined by Kathleen Dannemiller. The model describes the conditions that are necessary for change to occur and is based on a formula:

D x V x F > R

D – dissatisfaction with the status quo.

V – vision of a positive future state.

F – first steps to achieve the vision.

R – resistance to change.

D, V and F need to be bigger than the resistance to change (R) for change to occur.

This relatively simple formula helps managers to support teams through change by focusing on what is needed to overcome resistance to change. They can encourage the team to:

  • appreciate dissatisfaction with the organisation’s present ways of operating
  • build on a positive vision for the future
  • take the first steps to improve and change.

Once these have been consolidated, team members should feel more supported and confident about moving forward and will reduce their resistance to change.
