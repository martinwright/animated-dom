Change in the workplace

6 Examples of how to deal with negative impacts

The number of effects on people and teams that can occur when making changes is infinite. This table gives some examples of problems that are quite common, along with suggestions about how managers might deal with the problems.

Negative impact of change

Suggestions about how to deal with the problem

Staff hear rumours and are worried about consequence of the changes
Give accurate information as soon as possible. Reassure them and be honest about the action plan and how the changes will affect them. Give timescales about when more information will be available. Involve staff members in planning and evaluation processes. Provide confidential support and counselling
Staff make mistakes when following the new working methods
Reassure and support staff. Check that training is covering everything properly. Review quality control measures to make sure that the new standards are being checked correctly. Provide further training and support until staff are used to the new methods
New products, services and working methods are not as effective as they appeared in the planning stage
Listen to feedback and review the initial research that identified the need for change – amend as necessary. Amend the products, services or working methods to further improve them. Find a different way of marketing products and services. Review working practices regularly and look for ways to improve them. Communicate any changes and work hard to meet and exceed expectations

Managers need to remember that employers have a legal obligation and must consult and agree changes to existing employment contracts with employees. This means that they cannot simply change people’s job roles, terms of employment or working conditions without consulting them first.
