Change in the workplace

2 Negative impacts on individuals and teams

Negative impacts on individuals and teams can include, for example:

  • uncertainty
  • stress, worry and concern
  • conflict within the team.
Uncertainty

People naturally worry about how changes will affect them – eg possible redundancy or staff changes within the team. Dealing with bad news can often be easier than dealing with the ‘limbo land’ of uncertainty. At least we know where we stand if we are given bad news and we can get on with adjustment and looking for solutions. Not knowing the outcome of a planned objective just puts off the pain, keeps false hope alive, and leaves the ‘what ifs’ hanging around for far too long. Delays and lack of focus can cause debilitating stress for individuals, teams and organisations.

Stress, worry and concern

Team members can suffer from stress, worry and concern before, during and after a period of change. For example, they may:

  • feel inadequate — eg feeling unable to cope with new processes
  • fear losing control over their lives and environment — eg worrying about having to relocate or lose their jobs if the employer talks about closing a branch or factory
  • feel threatened by the possible loss of skills or competence — eg automating a factory in the manufacturing sector could lead to the downgrading of some jobs, plus possible redundancies
  • feel uneasy if the workplace culture does not embrace change — eg the philosophy is ‘the old ways are best’ or ‘we’ve always done it this way’
  • worry about rumours — eg if they hear about major changes or see stories in the media before they have been told officially
  • feel their views do not count and that no one is listening to them — eg if they are not given the opportunity to discuss options before major decisions are made
  • remember the failure or inadequacy of previous initiatives — eg failed attempts to cut costs or speed up production times.

Stress and anxiety can have a serious impact on teams and can easily cause physical and mental health problems – eg as a consequence of prolonged and high levels of stress and anxiety about changes.
