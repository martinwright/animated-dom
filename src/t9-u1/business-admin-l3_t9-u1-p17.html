Change in the workplace

2 Kotter’s 8-stage change model

Developed in 1996, the stages of Kotter’s change model are:

  1. Establish a sense of urgency – eg use open and honest dialogue to show what is happening and why change is needed.
  2. Create a guiding coalition – eg convince people that change is necessary; bring together a strong coalition or team of leaders from different departments to guide the team.
  3. Develop a shared vision – eg link the different ideas and solutions to establish a shared view; create a strategy.
  4. Communicate the vision – eg communicate the message and vision frequently and efficiently; address concerns openly; apply the vision to all aspects of the organisation; lead by example.
  5. Empower people to act on the vision – eg remove obstacles that may stop progress; review structures for change and remove barriers; identify people who are resisting the change and help them through the process.
  6. Create short-term wins to motivate with success – eg set smaller objectives that can be achieved, praised and rewarded.
  7. Consolidate and build on the change – eg avoid declaring victory too early; analyse each ‘win’ for success and areas for improvement; set goals to continue the momentum.
  8. Institutionalise the change – eg make the changes part of the core of the organisation; talk about progress; include changes within training and recruitment practices.

This model breaks down the stages of change into smaller parts, and these can be useful when a manager is supporting their team. For example, managers can support their team members through a period of change by:

  • being honest and open about the reasons for the change
  • guiding the team and developing a shared strategy
  • communicating the message and removing obstacles that may stop progress
  • setting smaller objectives and building on success
  • making the changes part of everyday life at work.
