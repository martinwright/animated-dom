Change in the workplace

1 Positive impacts for individuals and teams

Effects of innovation and change can be positive or negative for team members.

Positive impacts for individuals and teams can include, for example:

  • improved career development opportunities and better job satisfaction
  • better working practices and better job security
  • reduced stress
  • better teamwork.
Improved career development opportunities and job satisfaction

Change can present opportunities for team members to develop their careers and improve their job satisfaction – eg chances to take courses in new skills that are needed; from redesigned job descriptions, tasks and roles. The impact on the team generally can be better morale and higher levels of achievement.

Better working practices and better job security

When the organisation improves and updates its working practices, it generally becomes more efficient, competitive and profitable. Team members work to more modern standards that have been redesigned as part of the change process, leading to better efficiency and improved job security.

Reduced stress and conflict

Within the organisation or team, there can be a conflict of interest between different parts of the workforce who want different things. Also, there can be stress when working practices and resources need updating – eg when out of date machinery keeps breaking down or causes issues with quality; when procedures and policies are no longer efficient or fit for purpose. Changes in the team’s activities and working practices can upset people in the short term, however, stress levels and conflicts of interest tend to reduce once everything has been explained and updated, and things have settled down.

Better teamwork

Better teamwork is often a result of a group of people coming through a successful period of change together. The re-evaluation and reorganisation of team members’ tasks and resources can bring the team together, with a new appreciation of what each person’s contribution is. There is also a sense of shared pride from being a member of a successful and forward-thinking team or organisation.
