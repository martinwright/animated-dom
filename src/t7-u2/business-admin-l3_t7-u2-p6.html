2 PESTLE analysis

When developing strategies and plans, the senior decision-makers will evaluate and analyse the impact of all relevant external and internal factors. They may prepare a PESTLE and/or SWOT analysis. PESTLE stands for:

P – political

E – economic

S – social

T – technological

L – legal

E – environmental.

Collecting data in a logical fashion enables senior managers and directors to review the organisation’s vision and purpose, the internal and external influences, and which decisions they need to make to adapt to changes.

P – political

Government funding
Schemes to assist people who want to buy their own homes
Taxation, national insurance and pension contributions
Changes in the rate of VAT; tax charges for having a company car; tax relief for making contributions to employees’ pension plans
Interest rates
Making borrowing cheaper or more expensive
Staff remuneration costs
Changes in national insurance and employer pension contributions; paying the right salary for the job to attract the right people

E – economic

Recession
People and businesses generally review their positions and hold back on spending until things settle
Inflation
Increased costs of materials, wages etc; concern about how to ‘future- proof’ decisions to cope with possible inflation
Consumer confidence
Fears about recession and unemployment generally make people spend less; confidence in growth and having more disposable income can increase spending levels
Attitudes to work, leisure and family life
Changing attitudes and rights about taking time off work for family issues; changing working hours and location to improve the work-life balance

S – social

Education
Increased number of graduates; emphasis on engineering training to fill the demand for skills
Lifestyle changes
More people driving to work; effects of lifestyles on health and fitness
Demographics of the population
Dealing with an ageing population and gearing up services and products to satisfy demand
Increased online activities
More internet shopping than before; more services and forms done online

T – technological

ICT developments
Faster computers with more capacity to do more complex tasks; mobile devices that can do more tasks; increased processing speeds; greater network coverage improving accessibility
Production technique developments
Mechanisation and computerisation of tasks previously done by people; better or worse accuracy and reliability; shorter or longer waiting times for products or services
Employment law
Affecting working hours, rates and conditions
Health and safety laws
Amended to incorporate new technologies and procedures; simplified to reduce time-wasting health and safety myths

L – legal

Data protection law
To cover collection, use, storage and destruction of personal and confidential data
Food safety laws
To protect customers, staff and others from harm from the incorrect storage and preparation of food
Consumer laws
To ensure that consumers are treated fairly and that their rights are observed
Waste management regulations
Affecting the disposal of items such as fridges and cars; recycling targets; regulations to reduce packaging
Environmental protection legislation
Dealing with pollution of land, air and water; using environmental impact assessments to assess risks; encouraging and enforcing recycling, reusing and reprocessing

E – environmental

Energy consumption
Looking at the impact of leaving equipment on stand-by for long periods; vehicle fuel consumption
Ecodesign
Supporting the design and installation of eco-friendly equipment, affecting products such as boilers, air conditioning and water-using equipment
