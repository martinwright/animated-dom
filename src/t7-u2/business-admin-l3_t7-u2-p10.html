5 Pareto principle

The Pareto principle, also known as the 80-20 rule, is a method that can be used to assess efforts and activities to see which are the most productive. This can be applied to time management and many other aspects of business â€“ eg planning, decision making, leadership or project management.

General examples of the Pareto principle include:

  • 80% of output is produced from 20% of input
  • 80% of results come from 20% of effort
  • 80% of profit comes from 20% of the product range.

By documenting processes and procedures in an activity log, we should be able to identify the most productive 20% of effort that produces 80% of our output. By standing back and analysing this, we can plan how to improve our general output and efficiency.
