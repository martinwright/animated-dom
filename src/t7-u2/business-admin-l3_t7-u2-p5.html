Business process improvements

1 Business development tools

To undertake successful business growth and development, an organisation will need to understand its market or industry position. Therefore when developing business and organisations, it is necessary to analyse and assess the internal and external factors that have an impact on the organisation in its current position. This information provides a baseline on which the decision-makers can base their plans and measure the organisation’s progress and success. The organisation’s internal factors can include:

  • physical resources
    eg materials, equipment, machinery or premises
  • human resources
    eg team members, team leaders, supervisors, managers and directors; employees, contractors, agency workers or volunteers
  • time
    eg working hours, opening hours, delivery times or production deadlines
  • stakeholders
    eg shareholders or investors.

The organisation’s external factors can include:

  • external customers
    eg people who use or buy the products or services
  • suppliers
    eg offering different products or delivery options
  • world prices
    eg in oil, metal or minerals
  • external stakeholders
    eg government agencies or local councils
  • competitors
    eg offering different technology, working practices, customer service or special offers.
  • legislation and regulations
    eg about waste management or landfill tax
  • natural occurrences
    eg floods or high winds that affect supplies and deliveries
  • economic, political or social issues
    eg inflation and personal spending power, military action abroad, taxation and fiscal policies.
