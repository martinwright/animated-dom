5 Organisational policies, procedures, rules and regulations

Policies are formally documented and they specify what must be done in certain situations. They are usually written to meet various regulations and pieces of legislation, for example:

  • health and safety
  • training and development
  • equal opportunities.

Policies often give rise to step-by-step procedures that must be followed in order to carry out certain tasks.

Organisations write procedures to ensure a specific job or task is completed in a set way, guaranteeing the same outcome. It is good practice to have formalised and documented company procedures because they:

  • provide greater clarity of roles and requirements for staff
  • improve quality assurance and organisation
  • ensure consistency across the organisation
  • ensure legislative requirements are met
  • can be used in staff training.
