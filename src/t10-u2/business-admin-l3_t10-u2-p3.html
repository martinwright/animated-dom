Organisational structures

The term 'organisational structure' means how it is organised, for example, into branches, departments or teams. It determines how a company operates. The way an organisation is structured depends on its:

  • purpose
  • size
  • objectives and strategy
  • sector.

An organisation chart shows the structure of an organisation. It illustrates the relationship between those who work for the organisation and shows the chain/line of command, plus who is part of the decision-making process. Each organisation is different in the way it is structured and operates, and the organisation chart will reflect this.
