Organisational structures

1 Types of organisational structure

Organisations can be organised by:

  • function
    eg operations, marketing, finance, HR
  • region
    eg south, London
  • product
    eg in an electrical manufacturer: electronics, white goods, health and beauty
  • work teams
    eg client groups, project X.

Broadly speaking, structures are either hierarchical and flat, and either:

  • centralised
    decisions are taken by senior managers and then passed down the organisational hierarchy
  • decentralised
    authority is delegated down the chain of command.
