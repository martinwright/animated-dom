6 Understanding the organisational environment

It is important to understand the way that organisations respond to environmental forces because they:

  • help the management decision-making process
  • aid long-term planning of strategy and operation
  • give greater understanding of the influences that generate change
  • help to anticipate threats and opportunities and plan accordingly.

External forces will have an impact on organisations and the way they are configured. These forces can be grouped as:

  • Political — eg government tax policy, labour law, environmental law, trade restrictions, tariffs and political stability.
  • Economic — eg economic growth, interest rates, exchange rates and the rate of inflation.
  • Sociological — eg cultural and health aspects, population growth rate, age distribution, career attitudes and emphasis on safety.
  • Technological — eg research and development activity, automation, technology incentives and the rate of technological change.
  • Environmental — eg weather, climate, climate change and the potential impacts of climate change.
  • Legislative — eg discrimination law, consumer law, anti-trust law, employment law, and health and safety law.

A PESTEL analysis can be used to analyse how these different forces affect the organisation at different times and the difficulties involved in their responses to them. It sets out in table or diagram format the factors affecting the organisation under those six headings in order to decide whether they can be considered positive or negative.

It is a useful strategic tool for understanding market growth/decline, business position and operations potential, and is often used by organisations to assist them in building their vision of the future.
