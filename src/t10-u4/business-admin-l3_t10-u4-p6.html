Globalisation

'Globalisation is a process of interaction and integration among the people, companies, and governments of different nations, a process driven by international trade and investment and aided by information technology. This process has effects on the environment, on culture, on political systems, on economic development and prosperity, and on human physical well-being in societies around the world.' www.globalization101.org

Factors that aid globalisation:
  • trade
  • General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT – 1947 onwards)
  • free movement of people – basic pillar of the creation of the EU
  • 1992 Maastricht Treaty – abolition of internal borders and movement of people and goods
  • transport and IT developments are a major influence
  • inward/outward direct investment
  • emergence of foreign exchange markets/dealing in shares
  • spread of knowledge
  • movement of people – migration and immigration
  • technology
  • goods and services, such as imports and exports
  • capital.
