Introduction

When purchasing products and services, market forces will determine the choices that are available to the consumer, whether this is another business (B2B) or an individual customer (B2C). In sectors with a wide range of businesses offering similar products or services, eg international travel and accommodation, then there will often be wide price differences. In other sectors where there are limited businesses offering very specific services or products to limited numbers of potential consumers, eg space travel, then there may be lower price variances. The ability of a business to determine and influence the price of its products is largely dependent upon its market sector.
