7 Planned preventative maintenance

Planned preventative maintenance, as opposed to corrective (repair), is used to keep equipment in working order and/or extend its life. Through a programme of planned routine maintenance, it aims to avoid or mitigate the interruption to work activities that a breakdown or failure would cause.

The ideal preventative maintenance programme would forestall equipment failure before it occurs, through scheduled equipment servicing and replacement of parts. The benefits of preventative maintenance include:

  • prevention of unexpected equipment failure
  • monitoring equipment deterioration
  • reduced maintenance and replacement costs
  • reduction in system downtime
  • improvements/streamlining of services
  • equipment functioning at optimal levels.
