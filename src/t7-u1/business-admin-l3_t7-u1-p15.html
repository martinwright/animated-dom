Managing resources

4 Procuring resources

If you are responsible for procuring resources, you will need a system in place to monitor and record the quality of resources. You need to monitor the quality of resources continuously and make sure standards of service and product delivery are maintained. Problems such as damaged or faulty goods, failure to match agreed specification and non-delivery all need to be resolved with the minimum disruption to work.

Although you can never completely eradicate such problems, one way to minimise the possibility of them occurring is to select your suppliers carefully. Sometimes organisations have authorised suppliers but, if you need to choose a new supplier, you should evaluate their worth not only on the price offered, but also on their reliability and competence. Carry out desk research on the supplier to establish the ‘health’ of the company, for example examining their balance sheets, checking whether they hold any recognised BS/ISO competence standards, their record of strike actions. You should also carry out field research to evaluate past performance.

When selecting products/suppliers, you should take into consideration:

  • suitability for purpose
  • price
  • discounts available
  • terms of payment
  • delivery charges that may apply
  • after-sales service and any associated charges
  • reliability/reputation of the supplier
  • guarantees/warranty.
