Managing resources

2 Types of stock control system

A stock control system should be in place to monitor levels of stock held and ensure there are always sufficient supplies for effective working.

A common stock control system is the fixed order system, whereby a minimum and maximum level is set for each item, according to its use, to avoid running out and to avoid overstocking and thereby tying up finances and risking wastage through spoilage. The re-order point is reached when stocks fall to the quantity required for normal use during the lead time to delivery, plus a reserve amount to cater for any unexpected requirements or emergencies such as a delayed delivery.

Other systems are:

  • the periodic review, in which all stocks are reviewed and re-ordered periodically
  • the just-in-time system, which allows only enough stock for the next operation or project.

Provided that the system used is rigorously updated, analysis of the records should also give a good indication of levels of usage.
