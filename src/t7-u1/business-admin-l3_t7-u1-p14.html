Managing resources

3 Monitoring stock

Monitoring stock will enable you to identify differences between the actual and planned use of resources and to take action if necessary.

If you are monitoring stock levels, you should notice any significant variances between actual and planned use of resources and take prompt and effective corrective action, for example, changing planned activities, changing the ways in which resources are used for activities or renegotiating the availability of resources.

There will not be infinite funding for extra, unforeseen resource requirements, which is why it is so important to consider carefully all your resource requirements when planning for the budget period.

Running out of an important resource before the end of the period will mean going ‘cap in hand’ to senior management with a detailed explanation of how and why this has happened.

You must make sure resources are used efficiently, using historical and current data on resource use in your area of responsibility to compare and analyse the usage. Producing information in an appropriate form, such as graphs or charts, will help to identify trends and areas for improvement.
