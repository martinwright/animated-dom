Delivering projects

9 Kotter’s 8-stage change model

Developed in 1996, the stages of Kotter’s change model can be used as a tool and as an overall change management framework when managing a project. The eight stages are:

  • 1. Establish a sense of urgency
    eg when initiating the project and presenting the project plans.
  • 2. Create a guiding coalition
    eg convince people that the project has value and benefits; bring together a strong coalition or team of people from different departments to guide the team.
  • 3. Develop a shared vision
    eg create a strategy to deliver the objectives of the project.
  • 4. Communicate the vision
    eg communicate the purpose, aims and objectives of the project efficiently; address concerns openly.
  • 5. Empower people to act on the vision
    eg remove obstacles that may stop progress.
  • 6. Create short-term wins to motivate with success
    eg set smaller objectives that can be achieved, praised and rewarded.
  • 7. Consolidate and build on the changes made by the project
    eg analyse each ‘win’ for success and areas for improvement; set goals to continue the momentum.
  • 8. Institutionalise the change
    eg talk about progress; include relevant lessons learnt during the project within the organisation’s training and recruitment practices.
