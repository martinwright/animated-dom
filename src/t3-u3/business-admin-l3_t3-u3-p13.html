2 The appropriate use of social media in the workplace

Work is a big part of your life, so it is only natural that day-to-day experiences, good and bad, can filter into social media conversations. If your employer has valid concerns about the way their employees conduct their personal lives, such as breaches of commercial confidentiality or bringing the organisation into disrepute, such activities could count as gross misconduct, and even justify dismissal. This is the same whether the misconduct happens online or offline.
