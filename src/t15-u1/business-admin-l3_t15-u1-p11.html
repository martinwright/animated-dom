Producing business documents

1 Fit for purpose and audience

Your written communication must be suitable for:

  • the intended nature, purpose, reader
  • the type of information to be communicated.

It is important to know the purpose and audience of any information to be communicated to ensure you provide the correct:

  • degree of formality
  • level of technical content
  • depth of information.

Your audience may be internal or external customers, clients or staff. The audience will determine what content and language you use. Documents may be formal or informal depending on outcomes.

You may have to produce documents that:

  • describe
  • give information or instructions
  • persuade
  • argue
  • express an opinion
  • analyse.

The language used within the documents depends on the objectives that have to be met.
