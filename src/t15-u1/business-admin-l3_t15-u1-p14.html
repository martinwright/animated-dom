Producing business documents

4 Layout

Layout refers to the arrangement of the content within a document; this can be text, charts and graphics. The layout of a document can determine which points are emphasised, and whether the document is aesthetically pleasing.

Documents should have a flow: an introduction where you inform the reader of the reason for the communication; content where you discuss the matter in hand; and a conclusion where you sum up the communication and any actions.

You can format documents by using:

  • paragraphs
  • bullet points
  • font or sizes
  • tools and functions within ICT packages
  • margins and page breaks.

Most organisations have templates which set out the layout of documents; this will give the documents a standard approach.

A blank copy of the document is saved for future use over and over again.

Organisations use templates because documents are produced according to the house style (the standard look of a document) and because it saves time.
