Producing business documents

10 Data protection

In producing documents, you must be careful not to include personal information about third parties.

Personal data includes:

  • anything that identifies a living individual, eg name, contact details
  • anything that can be related to a living individual, eg unique account number, showing the current salary of a job post when there is only one person carrying out that job.

The Data Protection Act 1998 has been written to protect any living, identifiable individual from the misuse or unauthorised disclosure of personal data. The term personal data also covers any expression of opinion about the individual.

Organisations holding personal data must register with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioners and pay the current annual fee.

Under the Data Protection Act, data is classed as:

  • information recorded or processed by a computer
  • information recorded as part of a relevant filing system, or with the intention of being part of a filing system, or which forms part of an accessible record, eg health records.
