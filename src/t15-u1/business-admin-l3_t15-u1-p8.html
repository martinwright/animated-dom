5 Agendas and minutes

When a formal meeting takes place, there are two documents that are produced:

  • Agenda – lists the items to be discussed at the meeting. It gives those attending information to allow them to prepare for the meeting and gives a structure to the meeting.
  • Minutes – outline what was discussed. They act as a record of the meeting and record action points relating to work to be carried out.

Both minutes and agendas must detail the name, date, time and venue of the meeting as a heading.
