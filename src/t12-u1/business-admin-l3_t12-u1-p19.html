Organising meetings and events

1 Events and their purpose

There are many different event types, including:

  • conferences and seminars
  • annual general meetings (AGMs)
  • trade shows
  • training conferences
  • academic conferences
  • team-building events
  • awards ceremony.

These are just some of the events that businesses may run, however depending on the sector there are considerably more.

Conferences and seminars

Conferences and seminars are meetings put on to discuss a specific topic. Seminars are often recurrent and include the participation of the delegates.

The features of conferences and seminars are:

  • educational/informative
  • may have specialist speakers
  • presentations
  • delegates are invited or register to attend
  • they may run over one or several sessions.
Promotional events

These are based on selling something or increasing business awareness, such as trade fairs, book signings and fashion shows.

Exhibitions/trade shows

These provide an organisation or individual with an opportunity to show off their products or work. An organisation may pay a fee for a stand or stall at a trade fair. The size of the pitch required needs to be considered, as well as the staffing of it.

Delegates are likely to be moving around looking at what attracts their attention rather than sitting around a table.

Demonstrations of a new product, samples and promotional 'freebies', such as pens bearing the organisation name, will act as a reminder after the event.

Trade shows and exhibitions are:

  • used to showcase products and services that companies offer
  • usually not open to the general public
  • generally held in large exhibition centres
  • large events.
