Organising meetings and events

Organising meetings

There are two types of meeting.

Informal meeting – These are mainly internal. Notes are normally made but not using a formal layout.

Examples of informal meetings include:

  • departmental or team meetings
  • briefing meetings – to update staff on specific issues
  • progress meetings – to check on progression towards a goal
  • working parties – set up specifically to see a project or job through
  • one-to-one meetings.

Formal meeting – These follow set procedures in terms of documentation and processes involved. Key people who attend include:

  • a chairperson to run the meeting and have the casting vote
  • a secretary to take notes on what is discussed and agreed at the meeting
  • depending on what the meeting is about, there may also be a treasurer to put forward any financial points.

Examples of formal meetings:

  • health and safety meeting
  • annual general meeting (AGM)
  • committee meeting
  • board meeting.
