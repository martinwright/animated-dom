Organising meetings and events

7 Organising an event

Formality

It is important for delegates to understand if the event is informal or formal. Usually a business event will be formal, whereas an advice session will be informal.

Delegates/audience

It is important to know and understand the audience at the event and meet their requirements. For example, you wouldn't invite the catering team to a seminar on future rural development.

When sending invites, it may also be important to include a map of the venue, along with travel timetables and routes to help delegates find the venue and use public transport.

Activities

You will need to know if there will be activities during the event and what they will be. If you are organising a team-building day, then this will need to be organised and discussed before the event.

Length of session

The length of the event is important, not only for the venue but for the delegates, speakers, etc.

Presentation

The format and what will be presented is important. Also, the format of the presentation will need to be addressed before the event and planned accordingly.

Size

The size of the audience is a key factor when organising the event and looking at suitable venues.
