Organising meetings and events

9 Following the organisation’s procedures

Organisations often have procedures about how meetings should be run. These are the ground rules that everyone needs to accept and follow to make the meeting productive and effective. For example, procedures could ask people to:

  • switch off mobile phones
  • introduce themselves
  • avoid interrupting other people
  • stick to the agenda
  • not talk amongst themselves
  • start on time and stay until the end.

Some meetings are run on a formal basis where all questions go ‘through the chair’. This means that all participants have to address their questions and comments to the chairperson, then wait for the chair to give them their turn.
