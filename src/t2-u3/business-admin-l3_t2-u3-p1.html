Motivate and influence

Introduction

Leaders and managers often observe that motivating team members is the most difficult part of their role and ask why this should be? The easy answer is that people are different and what motivates one person may have little or no motivational appeal to another. Ken Blanchard, the well-renowned management guru and author of the One Minute Manager books, coined the phrase ‘different strokes for different folks’ when he talked about leading people. This message, whilst simple, is a useful pointer to how effective leaders and managers motivate their teams and the individuals within the team.
