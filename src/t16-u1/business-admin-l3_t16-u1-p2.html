Equality, diversity and inclusion

Key terms

  • Diversity — recognising individual as well as group differences, treating people as individuals and placing positive value on diversity.
  • Equality — the state of being equal, especially in status, rights or opportunities.
  • Inclusion — the idea that all people should freely, openly and without pity accommodate any person with a disability without restrictions or limitations of any kind.
  • Equality Act 2010 — the main law that serves to legally protect people from discrimination. It replaced previous anti-discrimination laws with a single act, making the law easier to understand and strengthening protection in some situations.   • Culture — the set of shared attitudes, values, goals and practices that characterises an organisation.
  • Prevailing — usual, common or widespread.
  • Inappropriate — not suitable or proper in the circumstances.
  • Role model — a person who serves as an example of values, attitudes and behaviours and distinguishes themselves in such a way that others admire and want to emulate them.
