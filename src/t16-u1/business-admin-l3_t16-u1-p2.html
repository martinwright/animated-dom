Equality, diversity and inclusion

What documents do you use?

  • Diversity
    recognising individual as well as group differences, treating people as individuals and placing positive value on diversity.
  • Equality
    the state of being equal, especially in status, rights or opportunities.
  • Inclusion
    the idea that all people should freely, openly and without pity accommodate any person with a disability without restrictions or limitations of any kind.
  • Equality Act 2010
    the main law that serves to legally protect people from discrimination. It replaced previous anti-discrimination laws with a single act, making the law easier to understand and strengthening protection in some situations. Before the Equality Act 2010 came into force there were several pieces of legislation to cover discrimination, including the following:
  • Sex Discrimination Act 1975
  • Race Relations Act 1976.
  • Culture
    the set of shared attitudes, values, goals and practices that characterises an organisation.
  • Prevailing
    usual, common or widespread.
  • Inappropriate
    not suitable or proper in the circumstances.
  • Role model
    a person who serves as an example of values, attitudes and behaviours and distinguishes themselves in such a way that others admire and want to emulate them.
