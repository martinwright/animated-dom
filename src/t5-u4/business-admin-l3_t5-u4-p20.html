Customer and stakeholder relationship management

3 Establish shared values for the relationship

People work and operate through values, or what we might call their ideals or beliefs about how they conduct themselves and their lives. If a person has a value of ‘achievement’ for example, and this is an important or higher-level value to them, they will aim to ‘achieve’ as part of their life’s goal.

This is the same for organisations and businesses. Therefore when building relationships with partners, establishing shared values between the two organisations will not only confirm that there is potentially a good ‘fit’ between them but also that when decisions are made and challenges are faced, the partnering organisations will act with the same values.

When organisations do not share similar values, there is the potential for a values clash. Values are often very deep-set and clashes can be fundamentally difficult to overcome.
