Managing challenging conversations

Introduction

People do not usually like challenging conversations and will often try to avoid them. We usually feel uncomfortable when we have to tackle someone about a difficult issue, especially if the conversation is face to face, and may feel awkward, embarrassed, nervous or afraid.

However, conflicts of interest, disagreements, complaints and disciplinary issues arise in the workplace from time to time, and we might be involved with difficult conversations when we work to resolve them.

Challenging conversations can take place between, for example:

  • two or more colleagues
  • members of different teams
  • managers or team leaders and their team members
  • managers of different departments
  • the organisation’s staff and its customers
  • different stakeholders – eg suppliers, shareholders, staff or customers
  • the organisation’s representatives and external agencies – eg HM Revenue & Customs or the local council.

Conversations can take place face to face, on the telephone, during a videoconference or when using other electronic methods of verbal communication.
