Customer and stakeholder relationship management

3 Negotiating with and influencing stakeholders

The skills used when negotiating with and influencing stakeholders are often used when solving problems and managing conflict. They include, for example:

  • win-win or collaborating — confronting and solving a problem by working with the other parties
  • forcing or competing — the individual firmly pursues their own concerns
  • smoothing or accommodating — the individual soothes others and accommodates their views
  • compromising — looking for a mutually acceptable solution that satisfies all parties
  • withdrawing or avoiding — ignoring or stepping away from the conflict.

We need to know the stakeholders well when selecting the most appropriate techniques, and use good communication skills and emotional intelligence to support and develop the relationships.
