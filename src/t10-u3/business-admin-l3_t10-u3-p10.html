Organisational culture and strategy

Organisational strategy

An organisational strategy sets out the organisation’s vision and purpose, upon which future, long-term developments will be based. It provides a framework for managers that informs the way they plan and deliver products and services, and how they manage their teams and departments.

The organisational strategy sets out the broad picture and will often cover several years. The details about resources, activities and performance measurements that will be needed to achieve the objectives set out in the strategy are contained within the operational plans for each team and department. These will include all aspects of the day-to-day running of the organisation as well as how team and departmental objectives tie in with the overall goals of the organisation.

Real-life examples can be found online by entering ‘organisational strategy’ into a search engine.

In this section, we are going to look at:

  • the process of developing organisational strategy
  • how operational plans relate to organisational strategy.

When we work out strategy at any level, the principles are based on the same four questions:

  • Where are we now?
  • Where do we need and want to be?
  • How do we get there?
  • How will we measure progress and success?

When developing organisational strategy, these questions still apply. We need to consider, for example:

  • Current internal and external factors that affect the organisation – where are we now?
  • The formulation of high-level strategy – where do we need to be and how do we plan to get there?
  • Translating the high-level plans into operational plans for departments and teams – the practical steps that will actually get us to where we want and need to be, and how we measure progress and success.
