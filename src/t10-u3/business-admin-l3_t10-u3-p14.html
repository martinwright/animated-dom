Organisational culture and strategy

4 Translating the high-level plans into operational plans for departments and teams

The high-level plans only contain the framework for managers to use when delivering products and services, and managing their team. The principles and elements need to be broken down into smaller components that are relevant for each department and team, and turned into operational plans.

The operational plans will be based on the information that the organisation cascades down to managers of departments and teams and could include, for example:

  • objectives for each team and department
    eg the output and quality that is required from each team; deadlines and how they affect other departments
  • new policies and procedures
    eg how to deal with new problems and queries from colleagues, suppliers or customers
  • updated standards and deadlines
    eg for customer service and delivery times.

Managers can then analyse and assess their own teamâ€™s current position and develop operational plans about how they are going to achieve their part of the organisational, long-term goals. They need to review, for example:

  • physical resources
    eg the stocks and supplies for each area of the workplace; machinery and equipment the team can use; updated offices for an updated department
  • human resources needed
    eg planned training sessions for current team members; recruitment and training plans for each department; planned agency staff and contractors to help the team
  • financial resources
    eg budgets allocated for each function in the department or team; the contingency funds that are available and how to request extra money
  • time
    eg different key stages and targets for each department and team
  • measuring progress and success
    eg new processes to measure output against forecasts; quality control procedures.
