Organisational culture and strategy

Organisational strategy

1 Current internal and external factors that affect the organisation

When developing organisational strategy, it is necessary to analyse and assess the internal and external factors that have an impact on the organisation in its current position. This information gives a baseline on which the decision-makers can base their plans and measure the organisation’s progress and success.

The organisation’s internal factors can include, for example:

  • physical resources – eg materials, equipment, machinery or premises
  • human resources – eg team members, team leaders, supervisors, managers and directors; employees, contractors, agency workers or volunteers
  • time – eg working hours, opening hours, delivery times or production deadlines
  • stakeholders – eg shareholders or investors.

The organisation’s external factors can include, for example:

  • external customers – eg people who use or buy the products or services
  • suppliers – eg offering different products or delivery options
  • world prices – eg in oil, metal or minerals
  • external stakeholders – eg government agencies or local councils
  • competitors – eg offering different technology, working practices, customer service or special offers
  • legislation and regulations – eg about waste management or landfill tax
  • natural occurrences – eg floods or high winds that affect supplies and deliveries
  • economic, political or social issues – eg inflation and personal spending power, military action abroad, taxation and fiscal policies.
