Organisational culture and strategy

2 Power culture

Handy uses a spider in the centre of a web to illustrate this culture, with ever-widening circles of contacts and influence. The closer we are to the spider, the more power and influence we have.

Organisations with this culture:

  • can respond quickly to events – due to the autocratic nature of decision-making and problem-solving processes
  • have control of resources as their power base – the people in the middle make all the decisions about how resources are allocated
  • are very dependent on key people in or near the centre of the web
  • attract people who are power-orientated
  • rely heavily on individuals rather than democratic committees
  • find it difficult to link too many activities and retain control – due to the rigid and autocratic management structure
  • can encourage intense commitment to corporate goals – by being so focused on the goals set by the senior person
  • can have a high turnover of staff who fail or opt out of a competitive, inflexible atmosphere.
