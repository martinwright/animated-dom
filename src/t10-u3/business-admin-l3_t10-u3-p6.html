Organisational culture and strategy

3 Role culture

The role culture can be represented by a building supported by columns and beams. Each section is a specific role and the role continues even if the people leave. For example, the Royal Air Force always has a Chief of the Air Staff, but the person in that role tends to change every three years or so.

Organisations with this culture:

  • have strong functional or specialised areas
  • have a high degree of formal procedures, standards and rules
  • do not encourage personal power as people are selected to perform particular roles
  • can be successful in a stable and predictable environment
  • find change difficult and slow
  • attract people who thrive on security and performing to set standards
  • do not attract people who are ambitious and want control over their work, except at senior levels of management.

Many public service organisations have this culture, especially as they are so large and concentrate on technical expertise rather than innovation.
