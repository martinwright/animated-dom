Project lifecycle and roles

3 Managers

Direction may come from the board, but the main planning, delivery and controlling of project plans will be the responsibilities of various managers. For example, there may be:

  • a senior project co-ordinator — with overall authority over budgets and important decisions for several projects
  • the project manager — the main manager for the project; the main point of contact for most decisions and problems; has overall control of everyday activities for the whole project
  • department managers — such as an HR or health and safety manager who are only involved with certain aspects of the project.

In organisations whose core business is project management, and in some other large organisations, there may be several full-time managers. Managers will deal with the different business functions and co-ordinate their activities with the project managers who have the overall view and control.
