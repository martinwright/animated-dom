Project lifecycle and roles

3 Timescales

When planning and preparing, the timescales need to be agreed so that all of the different elements can be brought together on time. Very often, one part of the project cannot go ahead until the previous stages have been completed, so a delay at any stage can delay the whole project or lead to its failure.

For our example, agreed timescales could include, for example:

  • starting the planning and preparation in January
  • booking bands and a large theatre for a concert date in October
  • having the booking office and website ready by February
  • launching the publicity for the concert in March
  • sending out tickets from May onwards
  • recruiting and training staff and volunteers to help at the concert between June and October.
